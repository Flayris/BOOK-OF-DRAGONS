Shellfire

General Overview

The Shellfire is a gigantic dragon belonging to the Tidal Class. It is an enormous deep-sea dweller known for its crustacean-like body, heavily armored shell, and devastating long-range firepower. Built for life in the ocean depths, the Shellfire combines incredible swimming speed with immense durability, making it one of the most dangerous aquatic dragons in existence.

Physical Appearance

  • Body & Color: It has a massive body protected by overlapping segmented scales that form a hard, carapace-like armor, giving it the appearance of a giant marine arthropod.
  • Head & Face: Its head features two long, slightly curved horns on the sides and a smaller horn on the nose, creating a fierce and heavily defended profile.
  • Appendages: It has four stubby but powerful legs that help support its huge body when it emerges from the water.
  • Wings & Tail: Its wings are long but proportionately small, serving more as swimming structures than true flight wings, while its body shape is streamlined for deep-sea movement.

Abilities and Combat

  • Plasma Charges: It fires cannonball-sized plasma blasts that explode with tremendous force, giving it devastating ranged attack power.
  • Durable Carapace: Its thick shell can shrug off powerful attacks, allowing it to endure heavy combat with little damage.
  • Protruding Horns: Its long prong-like horns are dangerous physical weapons that can be used to ram and crush enemies.
  • Deep-Sea Swimming: It can glide through the ocean at incredible speeds and dive to great depths with ease.
  • Red Algae Expulsion: It can expel red algae from its gills, adding another unusual defensive or environmental ability.
  • Immense Strength: Its enormous body allows it to tow massive structures and overpower ships and other sea threats.

Behavior and Personality

  • Intelligence: It appears highly aware of disturbances in its environment, especially when something intrudes on its back or territory.
  • Temperament: Shellfires are dangerous, aggressive when disturbed, and extremely difficult to challenge directly.
  • Behavior: They prefer to remain in the deep ocean and only rarely rise to the surface.
  • Social Traits: They are generally portrayed as solitary deep-sea dragons rather than social pack hunters.
  • Habit: When they surface, their armored backs can be mistaken for small islands or rocky landmasses by unsuspecting sailors.
