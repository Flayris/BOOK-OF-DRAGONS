Changewing

General Overview

The Changewing is a medium-sized dragon belonging to the Mystery Class. It is one of the most elusive and dangerous dragons due to its ability to become nearly invisible by changing both the color and texture of its skin. Combined with its corrosive acid attacks and hypnotic abilities, the Changewing is a stealth-based predator that relies on deception and ambush rather than brute force.

Physical Appearance

  • Body & Color: It has a sleek body covered in scales capable of shifting color and texture to perfectly match its surroundings.
  • Head & Face: Its head features expressive eyes that remain visible even when camouflaged, along with tendril-like feelers that enhance its sensory perception.
  • Appendages: It has long claws that allow it to climb surfaces easily, including vertical walls and ceilings.
  • Wings & Tail: Its wings are flexible and adapted for agile flight, while its long, prehensile tail can grip objects and enemies.

Abilities and Combat

  • Camouflage: It can alter both the color and texture of its skin, rendering itself nearly invisible in its environment.
  • Acid Blast: It spits highly corrosive green acid capable of melting most materials and remaining effective even underwater.
  • Hypnosis: It can hypnotize humans and dragons using its swirling pupils, forcing victims to obey its commands.
  • Prehensile Tail: Its flexible tail can grab, lift, and manipulate objects or prey with ease.
  • Climbing Ability: Its claws allow it to climb trees, walls, and ceilings like a gecko.
  • Speed and Agility: It is fast and agile, capable of dodging attacks and repositioning quickly in combat.

Behavior and Personality

  • Intelligence: It is highly cunning and strategic, often planning ambushes rather than attacking directly.
  • Temperament: Changewings are aggressive and territorial, especially when defending their eggs or territory.
  • Behavior: They rely heavily on stealth, remaining hidden until the perfect moment to strike.
  • Social Traits: They can work in groups when necessary, coordinating attacks for greater effectiveness.
  • Weakness: Their softer skin makes them more vulnerable when exposed, especially when their camouflage fails.
